Contributing to the Aurum Circuit

Thank you for your interest in contributing.
All contributions must follow these guidelines to respect the creator’s vision, safety standards, and ethical framework.

⸻

1. Respect the No-Harm Charter

All contributions must be:

• human-first
• non-weaponised
• supportive and protective in nature
• ethically aligned with the Aurum Circuit principles

Any attempt to introduce offensive, harmful, military, or coercive applications will be rejected.

⸻

2. Attribution Required

All contributions must include clear credit to the original creator:

“Based on the Aurum Circuit by Danielle Austin.”

⸻

3. Allowed Contributions

You may propose:

• diagrams
• non-commercial design suggestions
• accessibility improvements
• safety enhancements
• documentation
• research notes
• ethical analysis
• open-source collaboration ideas

⸻

4. Prohibited Contributions

The following are not allowed:

• weaponisation
• militarisation
• surveillance misuse
• violent or harmful applications
• attempts to override the ACHFL license
• attempts to remove or replace authorship

⸻

5. Pull Request Requirements

Before submitting a pull request:

• follow the ACHFL license
• confirm that your contribution is aligned with the No-Harm Charter
• explain your reasoning clearly
• describe the impact of your change

⸻

6. Commercial Development

No commercial or profit-based changes may be proposed or merged without written approval from Danielle Austin.

⸻

7. Code of Conduct

Contributors must engage respectfully and uphold:

• ethical behaviour
• transparency
• honesty
• collaboration
• safety-first thinking

⸻

These guidelines protect the integrity of the Aurum Circuit and ensure the project remains aligned with its human-first vision.
